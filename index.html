A Study on Wealth and Contentment

INTRODUCTION

The following is a relatively small, local study on the general relationaship between income and happiness. The analysis proposed here takes into consideration London boroughs only, focusing on a number of metrics such as mean and median incomes, as well as average happiness indices for each borough. The data used here was extracted from datasets kindly provided by the HM Revenue & Customs, and the Office for National Statistics (ONS), respectively. For more information, please head over to the acknowledgements section.

The following two visualisations were built for the study.

Income Variation by Borough:

This first tool consists of a line chart showing income variation throughout the years, for each borough in particular. Choose your desired borough from the dropdown menu on the left, and the chart will automatically refresh to display metrics for that borough. Moreover, options for mean and median incomes are available on the right. Note how, by changing these, the income scale updates as well, as median incomes are significantly smaller than the mean ones. Last but not least, by moving the mouse cursor over a particualr data point, a tooltip with the exact income value for the particular year is shown.

Relationship Between Income and Happiness:

This second, more complex tool, is a bubble chart showing the relation between income and general levels of happiness of people living in each borough. The (mean) income data is shared with the first visualisation, while average happiness indices were extracted from the ONS dataset discussed above. As opposed to the first chart, here the selection is based on years, and all the boroughs are scattered on the canvas at once, for each year. Note how the bubble areas are proportional to the number of tax payers for the associated boroughs. As before, hovering the mouse pointer over each bubble is going to provide the exact metrics associated with it. Last but not least, an optional regression line clarifies the overall trend of the relation.

Unfortunately, the City of London (not officialy a borough anyways) was exluded from this latter chart, as data with regards to happiness levels was insufficient.
